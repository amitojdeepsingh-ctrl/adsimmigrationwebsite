What is the Francophone Mobility Program?

Canada actively recruits French-speaking immigrants to support communities outside Quebec. The Francophone Mobility Program (FMP) is an LMIA-exempt work permit — meaning you don't need a Labour Market Impact Assessment to work in Canada.

This means faster approvals, less red tape, and a clear path from work permit to permanent residency — all because you speak French.

Why French Speakers Have an Advantage

1

LMIA-Exempt Work Permit

No employer needs to prove there are no Canadians to fill the role. Faster processing — typically 4–8 weeks.

2

Lower CRS Points Needed

Francophone draws through Express Entry select candidates with lower CRS scores than all-program draws, giving French speakers a significant advantage.

3

Faster Path to PR

French-language proficiency adds bonus CRS points. FMP work experience counts toward Canadian Experience Class (CEC) — one of the fastest PR routes.

4

Provincial Priority

Manitoba, Ontario, New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, and other provinces actively recruit French speakers. PNP nominations are often more accessible.

5

No Job Offer Required

You can arrive on an FMP work permit without a pre-arranged job in many cases — then find employment after arriving in Canada.

FMP Eligibility Requirements

1

French Language Proficiency

Must demonstrate French proficiency at the CLB 5+ level (TEF Canada or TCF Canada test). Higher scores = more CRS points.

2

Skilled Occupation (NOC)

Your job must be in a skilled category (TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 in the NOC system). Unskilled work doesn't qualify.

3

Valid Job Offer (for work permit)

For the LMIA-exempt work permit stream, you need a job offer from a Canadian employer outside Quebec.

4

Intention to Settle Outside Quebec

FMP is for French speakers who want to settle in francophone communities outside Quebec. Quebec has its own immigration system.

TEF Canada Language Test — What You Need

TEF Canada (Test d'Évaluation de Français) is the primary French language test for Canadian immigration. Here's how scores translate to CLB levels:

Aim for CLB 7+ (TEF 270+) for Express Entry. Higher scores give significantly more CRS points.
